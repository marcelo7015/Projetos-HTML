Linus Torvalds

The man who created Linux

Here is a timeline of Linux Torvalds:

  • 1969: Born in Helsinki, Finland.
  • 1988: He attended the University of Helsinki.
  • 1989: He joined Finnish Army Uusimaa.
  • 1990: He resumed his university studies and was exposed to UNIX for the first time in the form of DEC MICROVAX running ULTRIX.
  • 1991: The first prototype of Linux were publicly released.
  • 1994: Linux version 1.0 was released on March 14, 1994.
  • 1997: He moved to california and worked in a company.
  • 1999: He was named by MIT Technology Review TR100 as one of the world's top 100 innovators under age 35. In the same year Red Hat and VAT LINUX, both leading developers of the linux-based software presented Torvalds with stock options in gratitude for his creation.
  • 2003: He resigned and move to Open Source Development lab, which has since merged with the Free Standards Group to become the Linux Foundation, under whose auspices he continues to work
  • 2005: Torvalds received the Vollum Award from Reed College.
  • 2008: he was inducted into the Hall of Fellows of the Computer History Museum in Mountain View, California, "for the creation of the Linux kernel and the management of open source development of the widely used Linux operating system.
  • 2010: He was awarded the C&C Prize by the NEC Corporation in 2010 for "contributions to the advancement of the information technology industry, education, research, and the improvement of our lives".
  • 2012: Torvalds was declared one of two winners of that year's Millennium Technology Prize along with Shinya Yamanaka. The honor is widely described as technology's equivalent of the Nobel Prize. At Internet Society's Global INET conference in Geneva, Switzerland, Torvalds was one of the inaugural inductees into the Internet Hall of Fame, one of ten in the Innovators category and thirty-three overall inductees.
  • 2014: the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers named Torvalds as the 2014 recipient of the IEEE Computer Society's Computer Pioneer Award. The Computer Pioneer Award was established in 1981 by the IEEE Computer Society Board of Governors to recognize and honor the vision of those whose efforts resulted in the creation and continued vitality of the computer industry. The award is presented to outstanding individuals whose main contribution to the concepts and development of the computer field was made at least 15 years earlier.
